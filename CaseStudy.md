The Latin American food and beverage market is highly fragmented, with millions of small independent stores (“tiendas”) serving local communities. 
Grupo Mariposa partnered with McKinsey & Company to build a connected digital ecosystem designed to modernize how the company serves small retailers across the region.

Operating in more than 16 countries, Grupo Mariposa manages thousands of sales representatives, merchandisers, and delivery drivers serving a vast network of stores. 
The scale and fragmentation of this ecosystem created challenges in demand forecasting, route planning, order management, and store engagement.

To address these challenges, Mariposa and McKinsey built an end-to-end digital ecosystem powered by advanced analytics and connected technologies. 
At the core of this transformation is a platform that enables conversational commerce through tools integrated with WhatsApp, allowing store owners to place orders, manage inventory, and receive personalized recommendations directly through a familiar interface.

The platform leverages advanced analytics to analyze local demand patterns and generate AI-based order recommendations for retailers. 
Instead of relying solely on field sales representatives, store owners receive data-driven suggestions based on neighborhood-level consumer behavior and inventory trends.

Beyond ordering capabilities, the ecosystem also includes modular services such as microloans, loyalty programs, and customer-service tools. 
These capabilities help small retailers manage their businesses more effectively and strengthen relationships with the Mariposa distribution network.

Internally, the transformation digitized Mariposa’s commercial frontline operations. 
Sales representatives now use a mobile “atomic tasks” system that prioritizes daily activities, removes paperwork, and provides AI-generated order suggestions for each store visit. 
Delivery drivers also benefit from optimized routing and digital planning tools.

The transformation generated measurable impact:

• Over 100,000 retail points of sale connected through the digital ecosystem
• 8–10 percent sales uplift among stores actively using the platform
• 5,000+ sales employees equipped with new digital tools for commercial operations
• Improved order accuracy and inventory management through AI-driven recommendations

This case demonstrates how connected technology, advanced analytics, and platform ecosystems can modernize fragmented markets while empowering small businesses and strengthening supply chains across emerging economies.
